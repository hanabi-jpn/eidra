# Eidra Outreach Playbook

Use this page when sending direct messages to creators, newsletter writers, media, or early users.

## Outreach Goal

The first goal is not distribution.

The first goal is informed feedback from people who are already close to the problem.

Ask for:

- 10 minutes of testing
- one reaction
- one friction point
- one sentence describing what was clear or unclear

## Who To Contact First

### Warm contacts

People you already know who actively use AI coding tools.

### Builder creators

People who post practical content about AI coding, MCP, agents, or developer workflows.

### Security-minded developers

People who understand prompt leakage, tool risk, and trust boundaries.

### Newsletters and media

People who summarize developer tools, AI workflow trends, or security tooling.

## Rules For Good DM Outreach

- keep the first message short
- personalize the opening line
- ask for feedback before asking for coverage
- send one link, not a bundle
- make the ask easy to say yes to
- follow up once, not five times

## What To Send

The ideal first payload is:

- one sentence about Eidra
- one reason it matches the recipient's interests
- one link
- one simple ask

## Best First Link

- send [For Everyone](for-everyone.md) to general creators, educators, and non-technical audiences
- send [Use Cases](use-cases.md) when someone responds better to concrete examples
- send [For Developers](for-developers.md) to technical builders
- send [Media Kit](media-kit.md) to newsletters and media

## English DM Templates

### For developers and builders

```text
I am building Eidra, an open-source local safety filter for AI development.
It sits in front of tools like Cursor, Claude Code, Codex, and MCP workflows, scans what is about to leave your machine, and can mask, block, or route sensitive requests locally.
If you are open to it, I would love 10 minutes of honest feedback and just one thing that feels confusing or high-friction.
```

### For creators and newsletters

```text
I am working on Eidra, an open-source local-first safety filter for AI development workflows.
The short version is: it helps developers see what AI tools are sending, then hide, stop, or route the risky parts before they leave the machine.
This felt aligned with the topics you cover, so if you are curious I can send a short demo and the repo.
```

### For security-minded engineers

```text
I am building Eidra, an open-source proxy and MCP firewall for AI development workflows.
The focus is not "do not use AI." It is "put a local safety filter in front of it" so developers can inspect, block, mask, or route sensitive traffic.
If you have 10 minutes, I would really value one blunt reaction on whether the model and tooling boundaries feel right.
```

## Follow-Up Template

```text
Following up once in case the timing is better now.
If Eidra is not a fit, no worries at all. If it is, I would still love one quick reaction on the positioning or setup path.
```

## Suggested Tracker Columns

- name
- handle
- audience
- reason to contact
- link sent
- first message date
- follow-up date
- replied
- tried it
- feedback summary
- amplification potential

## What A Good Reply Looks Like

- "I tried it."
- "The setup was confusing here."
- "This is useful for X audience."
- "I still do not understand Y."

That kind of response is more valuable than early vanity reach.

## What Not To Do

- do not ask for a post in the first message
- do not send a wall of text
- do not oversell unfinished features
- do not use generic hype language
- do not send the same message to everyone
